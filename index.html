The company is defined by three production processes.

The clearest way to understand EVT-SOL is by its shop-floor flow: molded plastic parts are produced first, then routed either to wrapping or painting, and finally released through quality and finished-goods control.

INJ / Injection

Plastic interior part molding

Injection operations produce interior components and track production results, defects, line status, and mold shot counts.

IMG / Wrapping

Premium surface wrapping

Wrapping and graining operations give molded parts a premium interior surface while controlling fabric, color, step progress, and defects.

PNT / Painting

Automated painting flow

Painted parts follow a controlled process sequence with oven monitoring, quality gates, rework control, and final release.

How Production Moves

Two routes, one controlled manufacturing loop.

Wrapping and painting are separate production paths. Both begin after injection molding and both converge at inspection, finished-goods stocking, and shipment.

Route A

INJ -> IMG -> QC -> FG

Wrapped parts such as door trim and console surfaces move from injection into wrapping before inspection and finished-goods release.

Route B

INJ -> PNT -> QC -> FG

Painted injection parts move through painting, oven/process monitoring, quality control, and finished-goods stocking.

Control

Production data closes the loop

Work orders, defects, mold shot counts, oven conditions, inspection results, inventory, and shipment events are managed as one manufacturing system.

Quality and shipment are part of the manufacturing promise.

The process does not stop when parts leave the line. EVT-SOL's manufacturing story includes inspection, defect handling, finished-goods control, FIFO picking, and shipment confirmation.

QC In-process and final inspection gates before finished-goods release.
FG Finished-goods stocking and inventory control after production approval.
FIFO Picking discipline for shipment based on controlled inventory sequence.
OEE Production visibility across availability, performance, and quality.
