What Elevate makes, and how the parts move through production.

Elevate is a process-centered manufacturer for automotive interior parts. The core routing is simple to understand: injection-molded parts move into wrapping or painting, then through quality control, finished-goods warehousing, and shipment.

01

Injection Molding

Plastic interior parts are produced through controlled injection operations with POP production tracking, defect capture, and mold shot-count monitoring.

02

Wrapping / IMG

Surface wrapping and graining processes add premium interior texture using fabric roll control, PDA step checks, and defect management.

03

Painting

Painted interior parts move through a controlled multi-step painting process with oven monitoring, sequence control, and quality gates.

04

Quality & Shipment

In-process inspection, final QC, finished-goods stocking, FIFO picking, and shipment confirmation close the production loop.

Production Routing

Two main routings define the manufacturing model.

The MES design makes the business clearer: some products run through injection and wrapping, while painted interior parts run through injection and painting. Both paths converge into inspection, finished goods, and shipment.

01

Routing A: INJ → IMG → QC → FG

Door trim, console upper, and wrapped interior components move from injection to wrapping and final quality release.

02

Routing B: INJ → PNT → QC → FG

Painted injection parts move through painting, oven/process monitoring, quality control, and finished-goods stocking.

03

Closed-loop control

Production results, defects, mold counts, oven status, inspection outcomes, inventory, and shipment events are managed as one manufacturing flow.
